Key Qualities & Skills of an Effective CTO

The CTO as Strategy Catalyst

The CTO must understand the foundational drivers of the digital economy (SMAC + IoT), the exponential "laws" governing it, and the new rules of competition to drive strategy.

Drivers of Exponential Growth

Moore's Law: Computing capacity doubles every ~18 months.
Metcalfe's Law: Network value grows exponentially with users.
Bandwidth Law: Internet bandwidth grows rapidly.

Waves of the Digital Economy

First Wave (People-to-People): Focused on connectivity via SMAC (Social, Mobile, Analytics, Cloud).
Second Wave (Object-to-Object): Bridged physical and digital worlds via IoT.

The New Rules: Scale, Scope & Speed

The Rise of Ecosystems

Strategy is now about partnering to address customer needs.

Incumbents: Serve "Delivered Needs".
Digital Giants: Disrupt across industries.
Entrepreneurs: Address "Latent Needs".

Discovering Innovation Opportunities

To catalyze innovation, a CTO must first understand the firm's business model, then identify "sweet spots" for digital augmentation.

The 4 Components of a Business Model

Value Proposition: What customer need (delivered or latent) are we solving?
Critical Resources: What assets (brand, talent, IP) do we need?
Critical Processes: What activities must we perform well?
Profit Model: How do we make money?

Managing the Innovation Portfolio

A CTO must strategically balance investments across different project types and strategic horizons to ensure both short-term stability and long-term growth.

The Three Horizons of Tech Investment

Horizon 1 (70%): Sustain
Maintaining critical systems, optimizing cost and performance.
Horizon 2 (20%): Scale
Expanding capabilities and markets incrementally.
Horizon 3 (10%): Transform
Radical, disruptive innovation and new business models.
