Optional: Expanding Leadership Horizons

Leading Societies

Ethical leadership requires moving beyond mere legal compliance to create shared value for the business, its stakeholders, and society, guided by a clear sense of purpose and strong governance.

Purpose of Business: An Evolution

Then (Shareholder Primacy): The primary responsibility of business is to maximize profits for shareholders.
Now (Shared Value): Create economic value in a way that also creates value for society by addressing its needs and challenges.

Core Governance & Inclusion

Purpose > Strategy > Values: 'Why' we exist guides 'How' we deploy resources, bound by non-negotiable 'Guideposts'.
Social Inclusion: Fostered for ethical fairness and the business benefit of cognitive diversity.
Rigorous Audits: Proactive internal and external audits to identify issues early, not just for box-ticking.

Leadership Challenge with Sustainability

Sustainable value creation requires a holistic approach, balancing human needs with planetary boundaries and redesigning business models for circularity.

Circular Business Models

Shift from a linear "take-make-dispose" model to a regenerative one.

Reduce: Minimize resource use from the start.
Reuse: Extend product life via repair and refurbishment.
Recycle: Recover materials for new production.
Key Enabler: Product-as-a-Service Models.

The Three Scopes of Environmental Impact

Scope 1: Direct
Emissions from company-controlled sources (e.g., factories).
Scope 2: Indirect
Emissions from purchased energy (e.g., electricity).
Scope 3: Value Chain
All other indirect emissions, including product usage and disposal (often the largest).
